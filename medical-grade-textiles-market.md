<p><p>Medical-grade textiles are classified into three main types: non-woven fabric, woven fabric, and knitted fabrics. Non-woven fabric is made by bonding fibers together using heat, chemicals, or pressure. Woven fabric is created by interlacing vertical and horizontal fibers. Knitted fabrics are produced by looping yarns together. Each type of fabric offers unique benefits and is chosen based on the specific requirements of medical applications. These textiles play a crucial role in various medical products such as surgical gowns, wound dressings, and upholstery.</p></p>
