<p><p>Phytoremediation is a natural process that uses plants to clean up pollutants in the environment. There are several types of phytoremediation markets including phytosequestration, which involves storing pollutants in plant tissues; rhizodegradation, where plants promote the breakdown of contaminants in the soil through their root systems; phytohydraulics, which uses plants to filter water; phytoextraction, where plants absorb and accumulate pollutants from soil or water; phytovolatilization, which releases pollutants into the air through plant leaves; and phytodegradation, where plants break down contaminants through metabolic processes.</p></p>
