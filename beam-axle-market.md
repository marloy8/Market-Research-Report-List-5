<p><p>A dead axle refers to a type of beam axle that is not driven by the engine and is stationary, meaning it does not move independently. It is commonly used in trailers and some older vehicles. On the other hand, a live axle refers to a beam axle that is connected to the engine and moves along with the wheels. This type of axle is commonly found in trucks, SUVs, and some older cars. Both dead and live axles play crucial roles in suspension and support within the automotive industry.</p></p>
