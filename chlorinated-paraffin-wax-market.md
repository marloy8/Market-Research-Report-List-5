<p><p>Chlorinated paraffin wax is classified into three main grades based on the percentage of chlorine content - L-grade (chlorine content up to 40%), M-grade (chlorine content 40-65%), and H-grade (chlorine content above 65%). L-grade chlorinated paraffin wax is primarily used in metalworking fluids, while M-grade finds applications in leather processing and flame retardants. H-grade chlorinated paraffin wax is mainly used in PVC compounds, rubber products, and coatings due to its high chlorine content and flame-retardant properties.</p></p>
