<p><p>Acrylonitrile (ACN) is primarily produced via two different methods: the propylene method and the propane method. The propylene method involves the oxidation of propylene, a byproduct of oil refining, while the propane method utilizes propane as the main feedstock. Both methods result in the creation of ACN, a key ingredient in the production of acrylic fibers, plastics, and resins. The choice between the two methods is often driven by factors such as feedstock availability, cost, and environmental regulations.</p></p>
