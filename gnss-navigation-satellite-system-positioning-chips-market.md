<p><p>GNSS positioning chips are divided into two main types: high-precision and standard-precision. High-precision GNSS positioning chips offer greater accuracy and are typically used in applications such as surveying, agriculture, and autonomous vehicles where precise location data is crucial. Standard-precision GNSS positioning chips provide less accurate positioning information but are more cost-effective and commonly used in smartphones, wearable devices, and general navigation applications. Both types cater to different market needs based on the level of accuracy required for specific applications.</p></p>
