<p><p>Light-Emitting Diodes (LEDs) have various types including Organic Light-Emitting Diodes (OLEDs), Polymer Light Emitting Diodes (PLEDs), and others. OLEDs use organic compounds as the emissive electroluminescent material, providing high efficiency and flexibility. PLEDs use conjugated polymers as the light-emitting layer, offering cost-effectiveness and larger area coverage. Other types include micro-LEDs, quantum dot LEDs, and more. Each type caters to different applications and requirements, driving the growth and innovation in the LED market.</p></p>
